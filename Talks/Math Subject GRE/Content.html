Other Useful Tricks

  • Commuting differentials and integrals: \[\frac{d}{dx} \int_{a(x)}^{b(x)} f(x,t) dt = f(x, b(x))\frac{d}{dx}b(x) - f(x, a(x))\frac{d}{dx}a(x) + \int_{a(x)}^{b(x)} \frac{\partial}{\partial x} f(x, t) dt\]
    • Need \(f, f'\) to be continuous in both \(x\) and \(t\). Also need \(a(x),b(x) \in C_1\).
    • If \(a,b\) are constant, boundary terms vanish.
    • Recover the fundamental theorem with \(a(x) = a, b(x) = b, f(x,t) = f(t)\).
