We'll show a stronger statement, that $f(x) = \frac 1 x$ is uniformly continuous on any interval of the form $(c, \infty)$ where $c > 0$.

We can use that fact that 
\begin{align*}
\abs{f(x) - f(y)}
&= \abs{\frac 1 x - \frac 1 y} \\
&= \frac{\abs{x-y}}{xy}
.\end{align*}
