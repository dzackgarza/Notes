Definition of the derivative

%
$$
f'(p) = \lim_{x\rightarrow p} \frac{f(x) - f(p)}{x-p}
$$
%

Analysis

---

Definition of continuity
%
$f$ is continuous at $p$ iff

$$
\forall \{ x_i \}_{i\in I} \rightarrow p, ~\lim_{x_i \to p} f(x_i) = f(\lim_{x_i \to p} x_i) = f(p)
$$
%

Analysis

---

Prove that differentiability implies continuity
%
$$
\begin{align*}
f(x)-f(p) &= \frac{f(x)-f(p)}{x-p}(x-p)\\ &= f'(p)(x-p)
\end{align*}$$
and taking $\lim_{x \to p}$ of this expression shows $f(x) \to f(p)$
